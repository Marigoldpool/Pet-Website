Cortisol mixed with high blood pressure causes stress, which can lead to hypertension and high cholesterol. It’s that simple! Just cuddle with your feline friend and you will feel clam in no time!

Cons

Shedding

Shedding is a cat’s natural way of losing its unwanted fur. It may be annoying to see your feline friend’s fur an over the place but it’s a way for them to maintain a certain body temperature. In the winter and fall feral/wild cats tend to grow more and thicker fur to maintain a suitable and comfortable body temperature, while in the spring and summer feral/wild cats tend to shed a lot of fur off to keep from overheating. Well, old Daisy over there sheds no matter on the season or temperature of your house. Now if your feline friend is starting to have bald spots, this may be caused by a health problem that they have that should be checked out by your local veterinarian. some things that causes your cat to shed are: allergies, ringworm, bacterial infection, fleas, poor diet, stress, hyperthyroidism which is a type of hormonal imbalance, sunburn, pregnancy/lactation, and certain medications. If you think you cat has any of these you should take him to the vet to check, better safe than sorry! Ways you can minimize your feline friend’s shedding is to: groom regularly and feed him a healthy, balanced diet. If you don’t tend to your cat’s fur often it would lead to matted and knotted fur, which is painful for your feline friend, and can cause unwanted skin problems. Treat your feline friend to a nice spa night, that’ll keep him happy and you too!

Heat Cycles

Heat cycles occurs after onset puberty, which occurs in female cats at 6 months of age, a female cat will go into heat several times unless she is spayed. When this happens, she will become very aggressive and affectionate, and may exhibit behaviors such as loud vocalization and urine-spraying. She could end up with kittens if she encounters an unaltered male during this period. Unless you want kittens, please spay and neuter your cats!
